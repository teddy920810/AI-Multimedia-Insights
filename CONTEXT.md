# Writing Principles

## 1. Observation First

Always prioritize observations.

Good:

"I noticed an interesting pattern..."

Bad:

"Our product solves this problem..."

The goal is insight.

Not promotion.

---

## 2. Explain Industry Reality

Articles should explain how things actually work.

Examples:

* Why enhancement users behave differently from creators
* Why studio users differ from individual users
* Why benchmarks do not always translate to business success
* Why technical quality alone does not create a successful product

---

## 3. Avoid Product Marketing

This repository is not a marketing channel.

Products may be mentioned as examples.

Examples:

* Topaz
* Adobe
* Runway
* HeyGen
* ComfyUI
* Cloudinary
* HitPaw

However:

Articles should remain useful even if all product names are removed.

---

## 4. Prefer First-Hand Observations

The most valuable content comes from:

* Failed assumptions
* Product decisions
* User conversations
* Industry observations
* Ecosystem changes

Avoid generic AI-generated explanations.

Avoid repeating public documentation.

---

## 5. Practical Over Academic

Articles should be understandable by:

* Product Managers
* Developers
* Founders
* Creators

Avoid unnecessary academic language.

Avoid research-paper style writing.

# Language Workflow

All thinking should happen in Chinese.

All discussions with AI assistants should happen in Chinese.

All research notes should be written in Chinese.

All article outlines should be created in Chinese.

All first drafts should be written in Chinese.

Reason:

The author thinks and reasons in Chinese.

Using Chinese improves depth, clarity, and originality.

---

# English Publishing Workflow

English content should only be created after the Chinese version is finalized.

The English version should NOT be translated.

Instead:

Rewrite the article for native English readers.

Preserve:

* Ideas
* Observations
* Conclusions

Do not preserve:

* Chinese sentence structure
* Chinese writing habits
* Direct translation patterns

Target style:

* Medium
* LinkedIn
* Industry Blogs
* Developer Publications

The English version should feel like an original article written by an English-speaking practitioner.

# Final Rule

Every article should answer at least one question that is difficult to learn from public documentation alone.

If an article can be generated entirely from public information, it is probably not valuable enough.

The best articles come from:

"What surprised me after working in this industry."

rather than:

"Let me explain a concept."
